Frequently Asked Questions

We help businesses streamline their processes, enhance productivity, and achieve their goals through our cutting-edge technologies.

01What is two-factor authentication (2FA) and how does it enhance security?
Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to provide two different types of verification before accessing an account. This typically combines something you know (password) with something you have (a phone or security key), making unauthorized access significantly harder.
02What should I do if I receive a suspicious email?
If you receive a suspicious email, do not click on any links or download attachments. Verify the sender's email address and look for signs of phishing, such as misspellings or unusual requests. Report the email to your IT department or email provider.
03How can I ensure my organization's data is backed up properly?
Implement a comprehensive backup strategy that includes automated regular backups, storing copies in multiple locations (including off-site or cloud storage), testing backup restoration procedures, and documenting your backup policies.
04What is a VPN and should my organization use one?
A VPN (Virtual Private Network) creates an encrypted tunnel for your internet traffic, protecting it from interception. Organizations should consider VPNs especially for remote workers accessing company resources from public networks.
05How often should I update my passwords?
Security experts recommend updating passwords every 3-6 months, or immediately if you suspect a breach. Use strong, unique passwords for each account and consider using a password manager to help maintain this practice.
