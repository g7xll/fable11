FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about association governance, documents, meetings, and community standards.

Documents

Where can I find the governing documents?
Governing documents are available in the Documents section of this site and through the resident portal.
Can I request physical copies of the documents?
Yes. Contact the management office to request printed copies, which may be subject to a small fee.
How often are the documents updated?
Documents are updated as the Board adopts changes; the most current versions are always posted online.

Architecture

How do I submit an architectural or exterior change request?
Submit an ARC request form with plans, materials, and photos through the resident portal or management office.
Do I need approval for minor changes like painting my front door?
Many exterior changes require approval. Review the architectural guidelines or contact the office before starting work.
How long does the architectural review process take?
Most requests are reviewed within the timeframe stated in the governing documents, typically a few weeks.

Meetings

When are board meetings held?
Board meetings are held on a regular schedule; dates are posted on the Events page and the portal.
Can homeowners speak at board meetings?
Open meetings include time for homeowner comments, subject to applicable laws and meeting procedures.
Are meeting minutes available to all residents?
Approved minutes are made available to homeowners in accordance with the Association's policies.

Rules & Compliance

How are rules enforced?
Rules are enforced consistently and fairly in accordance with the governing documents and applicable law.
What should I do if I receive a violation notice?
Review the notice, correct the issue if applicable, and contact the office with any questions or to request a hearing.
How do I report a violation I see in the community?
Submit a report to the management office with details; reports are reviewed and handled confidentially.

Contact

Who do I contact for questions or concerns?
Reach the management office by phone or email; contact details are listed on the Contact page.
What are the management office hours?
The office is open Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM.
Is there an emergency contact number?
Yes. An after-hours emergency line is provided for urgent maintenance and safety issues.

Amenities

How do I reserve the clubhouse or other facilities?
Submit a reservation request through the portal or office, subject to availability and any deposit requirements.
Are guests allowed at the pool and gym?
Residents in good standing may bring accompanied guests, subject to posted rules and limits.
How do I get a key fob or access card?
Contact the management office to request or replace access credentials.

Assessments

How do I pay my association dues?
Dues can be paid online through the resident portal or by mail to the management company.
When are assessments due?
Assessment due dates are set by the Board and outlined in the annual budget and billing notices.
What happens if I miss a payment?
Late payments may incur fees and are handled in accordance with the Association's collection policy.

Maintenance

Who is responsible for landscaping?
The Association maintains common-area landscaping; owners maintain their own lots per the governing documents.
Who fixes street lights that are out?
Report outages to the management office, which coordinates repairs with the appropriate vendor or utility.
Does the HOA handle snow removal?
Snow removal for common areas is handled per the Association's policies and regional needs.

Pets

What are the rules regarding pets?
Pets are welcome subject to leash, waste, and nuisance rules outlined in the governing documents.
Are there any breed restrictions?
Any restrictions are defined in the governing documents and applied in compliance with applicable law.
Where can I walk my dog?
Use designated walking trails and the dog park; always leash pets and clean up after them.

Still need help?

If you can’t find the answer here, review the governing documents or contact the Association for guidance.
