Garcia v. TechStart Inc. — Wrongful Termination Settlement

July 15, 2025 · Maria Garcia (Plaintiff)

Carrington Law represented Maria Garcia, a senior Vice President of Finance at TechStart Inc., in a wrongful termination action arising from her protected whistleblower activity. Ms. Garcia had internally reported what she believed to be systematic revenue recognition irregularities to the company's Audit Committee — irregularities that, according to our expert's analysis, may have materially misstated TechStart's reported earnings for two consecutive fiscal years. Within sixty days of submitting that report, Ms. Garcia was terminated under the pretextual explanation of a "restructuring." Our employment litigation team was retained within weeks of her termination and immediately moved to preserve all relevant electronic communications, financial records, and board-level correspondence through an emergency litigation hold.

Building the case required meticulous reconstruction of the timeline between Ms. Garcia's whistleblower disclosure and her termination. Our attorneys obtained documentary evidence demonstrating that the stated restructuring had not been contemplated in any board minutes, budget projections, or organizational charts prior to Ms. Garcia's report. Depositions of TechStart's CEO, General Counsel, and two board members produced testimony that further undermined the company's stated rationale. Our forensic accounting expert identified specific entries supporting Ms. Garcia's original concerns, which bolstered both the good-faith nature of her complaint and the causal connection between her protected activity and the adverse employment action.

We asserted claims under the Sarbanes-Oxley Act whistleblower protection provisions, the Dodd-Frank Act, and applicable state employment law, pursuing both compensatory damages — including lost wages, lost bonus compensation, and diminished future earning capacity — and punitive damages given the egregious nature of the retaliation. TechStart's counsel initially offered a nuisance-value settlement that our team rejected without hesitation. As discovery progressed and the strength of our evidence became apparent, TechStart's negotiating posture shifted substantially. Mediation sessions conducted over three days ultimately produced an agreement in principle.

The $2.8 million settlement secured for Ms. Garcia represents one of the largest whistleblower retaliation settlements in the technology sector in the region for the year. The recovery included compensation for back pay, prospective lost earnings, emotional distress, and attorneys' fees. Importantly, the settlement also included non-retaliation provisions and a neutral reference commitment to protect Ms. Garcia's ongoing professional reputation. This result affirms Carrington Law's commitment to holding employers accountable when they unlawfully penalize employees for reporting suspected corporate misconduct in good faith.
