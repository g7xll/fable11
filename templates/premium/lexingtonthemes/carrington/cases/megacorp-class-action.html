Class Action Litigation

MegaCorp Class Action

March 20, 2025 · Johnson & Associates (Class Counsel)

Carrington Law served as defense counsel for MegaCorp in one of the most closely watched class action lawsuits of the year, representing a proposed class of approximately 85,000 current and former customers alleging deceptive trade practices and breach of consumer protection statutes. The plaintiffs, represented by Johnson & Associates, claimed that MegaCorp had systematically misrepresented the performance specifications of a widely adopted software platform, causing measurable financial harm to a nationwide consumer base. Our team entered the engagement at the motion to dismiss stage and immediately moved to challenge the adequacy of the class definition and the sufficiency of the pleadings on ascertainability grounds.

Our approach centered on two strategic pillars: aggressively contesting class certification while simultaneously building a comprehensive record on the merits. With respect to certification, we retained leading econometric experts who demonstrated that individual damages calculations would be highly variable and dependent on each class member's specific usage patterns — a showing that significantly undermined the predominance requirement under Rule 23(b)(3). Our briefing on this issue drew on cutting-edge circuit court precedents and succeeded in narrowing the class definition by nearly 40 percent, substantially reducing MegaCorp's maximum exposure before the parties engaged in mediation.

On the merits, our litigation team developed a comprehensive rebuttal to the plaintiffs' reliance theory, demonstrating through internal communications, marketing records, and third-party industry analyses that the performance representations at issue were materially accurate and consistent with established industry benchmarks. We also mounted a successful challenge to the plaintiffs' damages model, excluding their expert under Daubert on the ground that the model failed to account for non-actionable causes of any alleged underperformance. This exclusion substantially weakened the plaintiffs' ability to present a coherent damages case at trial and created substantial pressure toward a negotiated resolution.

The case ultimately resolved through a structured mediation process facilitated by a highly regarded retired federal judge. The final settlement agreement provided meaningful relief to class members while preserving MegaCorp's operational continuity and protecting its reputational standing with institutional clients. The settlement fund was structured to minimize MegaCorp's upfront cash outlay through a combination of product credits, service enhancements, and phased cash payments — a result that Carrington's negotiating team worked diligently to achieve. The court granted final approval following a fairness hearing, and the settlement was acknowledged by industry observers as a benchmark outcome for consumer class actions in the technology sector.
