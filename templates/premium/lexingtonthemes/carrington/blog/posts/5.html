Carrington Law Partners with Local Nonprofits

Carrington Law is pleased to announce formal pro bono partnerships with three prominent nonprofit organizations: the Legal Aid Society of New York, the Immigrant Justice Initiative, and the Small Business Legal Assistance Network. These partnerships reflect Carrington's long-standing commitment to providing legal services to underserved communities and represent the firm's most structured commitment to pro bono work in its history. Under the agreements, attorneys from each of Carrington's practice groups will dedicate a minimum of 60 hours per year to matters referred through the partner organizations, with the goal of exceeding 5,000 hours of pro bono service firmwide during the first year of the program.

The partnership with the Legal Aid Society will primarily focus on civil matters involving housing, family law, and consumer debt. Carrington attorneys will assist Legal Aid clients with eviction defense, guardianship proceedings, and negotiations with creditors — areas where professional legal representation can make a decisive difference in outcomes but where clients typically cannot afford private counsel. Several Carrington partners with backgrounds in real estate litigation and family law have already completed intake training and will begin accepting referrals in the coming weeks.

Through the Immigrant Justice Initiative, Carrington attorneys will assist asylum seekers, DACA recipients, and undocumented immigrants facing deportation proceedings. The firm's existing expertise in international law and cross-border transactions provides a natural foundation for this work, and a number of associates with language skills in Spanish, Mandarin, and Portuguese have volunteered to participate. The firm has also committed to funding translation services for clients who require assistance in languages not covered by its attorney roster.

The Small Business Legal Assistance Network partnership will channel Carrington's corporate, employment, and IP expertise toward entrepreneurs and small business owners who lack access to affordable legal services. Attorneys will help clients with entity formation, contract review, trademark registration, employment agreement drafting, and commercial lease negotiations — matters that can have outsized consequences for small businesses but that are often addressed without legal guidance due to cost constraints. Carrington is proud to deepen its roots in the communities it serves and views these partnerships as an expression of its core values.
