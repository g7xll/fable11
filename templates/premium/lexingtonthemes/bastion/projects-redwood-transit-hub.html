Project overview

Redwood Transit Authority commissioned a new multimodal transit hub to serve Seattle's growing commuter population. The facility integrates rail, bus, and retail into a single high-capacity structure engineered for continuous heavy daily usage.

Scope of work

Our scope covered the full construction cycle from foundation through handover, including structural works, MEP coordination, platform construction, and interior fit-out for retail concourse areas. We managed multiple subcontractor teams simultaneously across overlapping construction phases.

  • Site preparation and deep foundation works
  • Structural steel and concrete superstructure
  • Rail platform and bus bay construction
  • MEP systems including HVAC, electrical, and plumbing
  • Interior fit-out for retail and passenger waiting areas
  • Wayfinding, safety systems, and accessibility compliance

Deliverables

The completed hub provides rail and bus connections for over 60,000 daily riders, with integrated retail space and full accessibility compliance. All mechanical and electrical systems were commissioned and tested prior to handover.

Key highlights

  • 22-month delivery from groundbreaking to handover
  • 98% on-time milestone completion across all construction phases
  • Capacity engineered for 60,000 daily riders at peak load
  • Zero lost-time safety incidents across the project
  • Coordinated live utility diversions during active construction phases
