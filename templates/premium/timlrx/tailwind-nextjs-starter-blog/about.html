About Me

I am a Professor of Atmospheric Science at Stanford University. I lead the clean energy group which focuses on applying machine learning methods to tackle climate change and making clean energy more accessible.

My research group develops climate-scale models and uses differential equations to model the atmosphere. Our most recent project involves modeling tailwinds, headwinds, and crosswinds with complexity modelling. In our lab we have developed a 3D air pollution-climate model and differential equation solvers.

In my spare time I love cooking, hiking, and baking. I bake a mean sourdough and have recently got into making homemade pasta. I also enjoy reading, and have recently been reading The Pragmatic Programmer and Clean Code.

Research

My research focusses on the atmospheric physics of tropical clouds and convection and the role of moisture in tropical climate change. I primarily use theoretical methods and simple models to understand large-scale atmospheric dynamics, tropical meteorology, and the global water cycle. I am also interested in machine learning methods as they apply to atmospheric and climate science.
