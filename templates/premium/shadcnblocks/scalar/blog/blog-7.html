From markdown to structured content

Why content teams are moving away from flat files and free-form markdown toward structured data — and how Scalar makes the switch painless.

Rob Austin · 5 min read

Markdown files are a great way to start, but they don't scale. As your content grows, free-form documents become hard to query, reuse, and validate. Structured content fixes that — here's why teams are making the move.

The limits of markdown

A markdown file mixes content and presentation, has no schema, and can't be safely reused across channels. Want every post to have an author and a hero image? With markdown there's nothing stopping a writer from forgetting either.

  • No validation — required fields can simply be missing.
  • No relationships — linking an author means copy-pasting their name.
  • No reuse — the same content can't power a web page and a mobile app.

What structured content gives you

Structured content separates data from presentation. Each piece is a typed record with validated fields and real relationships you can query.

post.json
{ "title": "Hello world", "author": "ref:author/rob", "body": [{ "type": "paragraph" }] }

Migrating without the pain

Scalar ships an importer that parses your existing markdown frontmatter into typed fields and converts the body to portable rich text. Run it once and your archive is structured.

The importer is idempotent — run it as many times as you need while tuning your field mappings.

Reusing content everywhere

Once content is structured, the same record powers your website, app, and newsletter — each consuming only the fields it needs.

Wrap-up

Markdown is a fine starting point, but structured content is how you scale. Scalar makes the migration painless and the payoff lasting.
